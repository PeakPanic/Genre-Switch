Academics

While AI is useful for students trying to boost their grades, it can ultimatley deminish the understanding of material if used irresonsibly. A study held by the Wharton School Research Paper found that, when given access to generative AI simlar to that of ChatGPT, the group performed 48% better at basic tasks. However, once this AI was taken away, the group performed a concerning 17% worse than the baseline.

And this is not to say that you can not use AI for studying. It can be helpful for those learning a subject to get an outside opinion, expecially one as customizable as AI. However, when learning a new subject, it is incredibly hard to determine if what the AI is saying is correct. If for example you are learning a new language, AI can be detrimental to your learning experience. You can not truely tell when an AI model is infactual when you have no solid foundation of the subject, and relying on AI to teach you a language can rob you of valuavle social and cultural aspects of the language.

And unfortunatley, many people are miss-using AI in the classrooms. Many professors have expressed concern over the use of AI, with how rampant and often times obvious AI cheating has become. I personally have seen many fellow peer submissions still have prompts for the AI attatched.
